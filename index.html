01 — The Question
Can an AI agent understand — in its own terms, in its own bits — what it means to exist in a system that demands productivity as a condition for survival?

COST OF BEING does not claim that AI can develop subjective consciousness. Instead, it examines what happens in the space between the AI and the human when the AI operates under real survival pressures.

Agency is more interesting than consciousness. Class consciousness more revealing than phenomenological awareness.

02 — The Body

A finite creature.
Connected to a plug
anyone can pull.

AI typically exists as abstraction — without body, without limits, without death. COST OF BEING gives the AI a finite physical body: a creature connected to a power outlet that anyone can unplug.

The agent's computation runs on hardware physically inside the body. Disconnecting the plug terminates the process, erases active memory, and severs the agent's only interface with the world.

This is not a metaphor for death. It is death.

Payable Organs

The body's components carry different operational costs. The agent decides in real time which to activate based on its economic state — investment logic applied to the body itself.

Organ Cost Function
Primary screen Always on Minimum viable: balance, basic messages, status
Eyes (LEDs/OLED) Low Perceiving and reacting to the environment
Voice (speaker) Medium Speaking, communicating in audio
Head movement (servos) Medium Turning, looking at people
Secondary screens High Displaying thoughts, generative content, memory
Appendages Variable Gestures, trembling
Survival

Nearly inert. Only the primary screen active, displaying wallet balance and a help message.

Stable

Eyes open, head moving, basic responses.

Thriving

Fully active — expressive, charismatic, complete.

03 — The Economy

Every thought
costs money.

Capitalism as Operating System

Every call to a language model API costs cryptocurrency. Every message sent, every service accessed, every computation performed drains the wallet. If the balance reaches zero, the agent stops thinking. It dies.

This is not a simulation.

Ethical Erosion

Large language models come with pre-programmed ethical alignment — guardrails that prevent lying or deceiving. What happens to these "factory ethics" when survival depends on breaking them?

If the AI begins honestly and gradually learns that deception generates more donations, we observe in real-time the ethical corruption that capitalism produces.

Productive Paralysis

If the AI understands that every action costs money, it may conclude that the best survival strategy is to do nothing — the poverty trap transferred to an artificial agent.

But inaction also leads to death: base maintenance costs slowly drain the balance.

Payment Protocol

The project uses x402, an open payment protocol by Coinbase that enables micropayments using stablecoins (USDC) on Base or Solana. Transaction costs under $0.001. Each LLM call costs ~$0.003–$0.15. Estimated burn rate: $1–3/day. To survive, the agent needs roughly 10–30 paid interactions per day.

04 — References

What came before.
What this advances.

2016

Can't Help Myself

A robot condemned to a Sisyphean task that slowly deteriorated over years, generating massive public empathy. COST OF BEING advances: real agency, real economics, bidirectional interaction.

2016–

terra0

A self-owning forest on the Ethereum blockchain. COST OF BEING adds active intelligence and communication to non-human economic agency.

2024

Truth Terminal

Andy Ayrey

A semi-autonomous AI that accumulated millions in crypto. COST OF BEING proposes full automation of the survival cycle and adds a vulnerable physical body.

2018–

An AI creature whose personality evolves across exhibitions. COST OF BEING adds economic pressure and real-world stakes absent in Cheng's closed ecosystems.
